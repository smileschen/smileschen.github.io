Cost of attendance

A UCLA graduate program with flexible quarterly costs.

MASDS tuition is charged by the unit. If work gets busy and you take fewer courses in a quarter, your tuition for that quarter is lower because you are enrolled in fewer units.

These estimates cover program tuition and student fees. Housing, food, books and supplies, personal expenses, parking, transportation, and other living costs are not included.

Looking for last year's figures? View 2025–2026 tuition and fees.

2026–27 tuition rate

Tuition for the MASDS program is charged at $1,267 per unit for Academic Year 2026–27. Each standard course is 4 units, so tuition for one course is $5,068. Students must also pay student services fees, campus-based fees, UCSHIP health insurance unless waived, and other required fees.

Regular UCLA graduate tuition and nonresident supplemental tuition do not apply to the MASDS program; MASDS uses its own per-unit program tuition.

MASDS tuition is cohort-based. If program tuition increases in a future year, the increase applies to future entering students; a student's MASDS tuition rate remains locked at the price in effect when they entered the program. UCLA student fees and campus-based fees may still change.

More than tuition: resources included with UCLA enrollment

MASDS students can access UCLA technology, research, library, transportation, and recreation resources.

Technology and productivity
  • O’Reilly for Higher Education provides access to more than 38,000 computer science and business titles, plus video courses.
  • Overleaf Professional provides collaborative writing and LaTeX tools.
  • Adobe Creative Cloud includes applications such as Photoshop, Illustrator, Acrobat, and Premiere Pro.
  • Microsoft Office 365 Education available for registered UCLA students.
  • UCLA Box provides unlimited cloud storage for enrolled students.
Research and computing
  • Hoffman2 high-performance computing cluster supports advanced research computing. A UCLA faculty or research sponsor is required.
  • UCLA Library online resources include subscribed journals, books, and databases from SAGE, Wiley, SpringerLink, and other publishers. Coverage varies by title and license.
  • Graduate Writing Center offers writing workshops and thesis support.
Transportation and recreation
  • Bruin Grad Pass provides eligible graduate students with access to LA Metro, LADOT Commuter Express, Santa Monica Big Blue Bus, plus more.
  • UCLA Recreation includes facilities such as the John Wooden Center, Kinross Recreation Center, pools, stadium facilities, and tennis courts.

Availability and eligibility vary; some resources require account activation, applicable campus fees, or current-quarter enrollment.

Planning your course load

The typical workload is seven core courses plus four electives. Each course is 4 units, and students must complete 44 units total.

Two or three courses per quarterStudents who take two 4-unit courses per quarter typically finish the program in spring of their second year. Students may take up to three courses in a single quarter to finish faster, but this heavy course load is not recommended for students who work full time.
One course per quarterWhen work or life gets busy, students may take one 4-unit course and continue progressing at a more flexible pace.
Course waiversStudents may request waivers for up to three core courses by showing equivalent graduate-level coursework. Waived core courses must be replaced with electives to satisfy the 44-unit requirement.
