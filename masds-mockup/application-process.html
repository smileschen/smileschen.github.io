Application process

Apply with a clear sense of what we look for.

We welcome applicants from a range of academic and professional backgrounds. A bachelor's degree is required, but a statistics major is not.

1

Confirm your preparation

Strong applicants typically have quantitative preparation in linear algebra, multivariable calculus, probability, statistics, and programming. If your undergraduate degree is not in statistics, preparation should include calculus, linear algebra, statistics, probability, and basic programming with R.

2

Prepare application materials

Applicants submit transcripts, a statement of purpose, a personal statement, a resume or CV, and two letters of recommendation through UCLA's graduate application system.

3

Highlight professional experience

The program values documented work experience, especially applying quantitative methods in research, business, or other professional settings.

4

Submit through UCLA Graduate Admission

All applicants apply online through UCLA's Application for Graduate Admission. Use Major Code 00J3 for MASDS.

GRE scoresThe GRE is highly suggested but not required. High GRE scores may strengthen an application.

Additional admissions notes

These details help applicants plan timing, testing, and eligibility before submitting an application.

Application reviewApplications are reviewed once per year for Fall quarter. Decisions may be released between late February and early April, depending on application volume.
International applicantsThe program may accept international students and sponsor F-1 or J-1 visas. International students typically must enroll in 8 units, or two courses, per academic quarter.
English proficiencyApplicants whose first language is not English must certify English proficiency through TOEFL or IELTS unless eligible for a waiver.
STEM designationMASDS is a STEM program in the Division of Physical Sciences. Applied Statistics is listed with CIP code 27.0600.
Financial assistanceApplicants are considered for fellowship assistance. Current program information notes full-tuition awards for a portion of admitted students, contingent on academic performance.
One application policyUCLA Graduate Division policy permits applicants to apply to one graduate program per application period.
