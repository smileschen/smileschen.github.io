Application process

Apply with a clear sense of what we look for.

We welcome applicants from a range of academic and professional backgrounds. A bachelor's degree is required, but an undergraduate degree in statistics is not.

Start application UCLA application steps
1

Confirm your preparation

Applicants should have taken courses in linear algebra, multivariable calculus, and probability and statistics. Applicants should also have experience in computer programming; any programming language is acceptable. MASDS courses are taught in R and Python.

If the undergraduate degree is not in STEM, the application should show evidence of coursework in the areas listed above.

2

Prepare application materials

Applicants submit transcripts, a statement of purpose, a personal statement, a resume or CV, GRE scores, and two letters of recommendation through UCLA's graduate application system.

3

Highlight professional experience

The program values documented work experience, especially applying quantitative methods in research, business, or other professional settings.

4

Submit through UCLA Graduate Admission

All applicants apply online through UCLA's Application for Graduate Admission. Use Major Code 00J3 for MASDS.

Required materials

Use this checklist to plan your application before submitting through the UCLA Graduate Division system.

Academic recordsUpload copies of transcripts during the application period. Official records are required after admission.
StatementsSubmit both a statement of purpose and a personal statement describing preparation, interests, and career goals.
Resume or CVShowcase professional experience, quantitative work, research, projects, awards, and honors.
RecommendationsTwo letters are required from people familiar with your academic or professional performance. A third letter will be reviewed if provided.
GRE scoresGRE scores are required for admission. GRE scores are valid for five years. If you take a particular GRE test more than once, the Admissions Committee will accept the highest score, but it will have access to all submitted scores.
Application feeApplication fees are set by UCLA Graduate Division and are nonrefundable.

Additional admissions notes

These details help applicants plan timing, testing, and eligibility before submitting an application.

Application reviewApplications are reviewed once per year for Fall quarter. Decisions may be released between late February and early April, depending on application volume.
International applicantsThe program may accept international students and sponsor F-1 or J-1 visas. International students typically must enroll in 8 units, or two courses, per academic quarter.
English proficiencyApplicants whose first language is not English must certify English proficiency through TOEFL or IELTS unless eligible for a waiver.
STEM designationMASDS is a STEM program in the Division of Physical Sciences. Applied Statistics is listed with CIP code 27.0600.
Financial assistanceApplicants are considered for fellowship assistance. Current program information notes full-tuition awards for a portion of admitted students, contingent on academic performance.
One application policyUCLA Graduate Division policy permits applicants to apply to one graduate program per application period.
