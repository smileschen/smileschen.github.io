Industry partners

Connect classroom learning with real-world data problems.

MASDS emphasizes applied work, professional networking, and thesis projects that may involve industrial, business, and government partners who propose problems, provide data, and advise students alongside UCLA faculty.

How industry engagement supports students

In-person learning makes professional connection more natural: students meet peers, faculty, alumni, guest speakers, and industry professionals throughout the program.

Networking eventsIndustry nights, guest speakers, employer visits, and alumni panels give students opportunities to meet professionals working with data.
Applied thesis projectsThesis topics often originate from industry partners who provide real-world problems and data sets.
Internship accessStudents are encouraged to participate in internships with industry partners and may earn approved elective credit for substantial internship-related work.

For partner organizations

Work with students on original analysis with faculty oversight.

Industry partners may guide the program, sponsor student thesis projects, provide access to internship programs, and work with faculty and students on analysis that addresses real scientific or business problems.

Faculty advisers ensure the statistical integrity of the work. When students work with proprietary data, partners must agree that the student can publish and distribute the thesis.
