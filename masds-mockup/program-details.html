Program details

Graduate training in statistical thinking, data science, and applied problem solving.

For students entering in Fall 2024 or later, MASDS students complete a minimum of 44 units through seven required core courses, four electives, and a written thesis based on original analysis.

44 unitsMinimum course credit required for the MASDS degree.
7 core coursesRequired graduate courses in statistical theory, methods, computing, data management, and communication.
4 electivesAt least four 4-unit electives chosen under the Fall 2024-or-later program rules.

Flexible pacing for working professionals

MASDS is designed for students who are balancing graduate study with professional responsibilities. Students may take up to two courses per quarter, including summer, to complete their 11 total courses.

Complete in about two yearsStudents who take up to two courses per quarter, including summer, typically complete the program in 21 months.
Adjust when work gets busyStudents can take one course in heavier professional periods and continue moving through the program at a manageable pace.
Finish within program limitsStudents may take up to 10 regular quarters to complete the program without additional approval. Summer quarters do not count toward the 10-quarter limit, so this is about three years and one quarter. Students who need more time may be approved on a case-by-case basis.

Elective requirements for Fall 2024 or later

Students complete four elective courses, totaling 16 units. Electives must be Statistics 400-level courses or specified 200-level courses that meet program restrictions.

Four 4-unit electivesEach elective course is 4 units. Students complete at least four electives in addition to the seven core courses.
Internship electiveStatistics 496 may count for up to 4 units of elective credit if approved by the MASDS Director.
Individual study electiveStatistics 497 may count for up to 4 units toward the required electives.
Thesis researchStatistics 498 Master’s Thesis Research courses may not count as an elective.
Selected 200-level coursesMASDS students may take specified 200-level courses between 203 and 283, subject to restrictions, approval, and space availability.
Outside enrollmentStudents outside the MASDS program may not take MASDS 400-level courses.

Course waivers

Students entering under the newer curriculum may request waivers when they have already completed equivalent graduate-level coursework.

Up to three core coursesA maximum of three core courses may be waived by request.
Reviewed by the programThe Director, with consultation from the MASDS governance committee, reviews transcripts, syllabi, and other relevant materials.
44 units still requiredFor every course waived, the student must complete additional elective coursework to satisfy the 44-unit degree requirement.

Thesis requirement

Every student completes original analysis on a real-world problem.

The MASDS thesis reports on research and analysis completed under the supervision of a UCLA faculty member and an industry partner when available. Thesis topics often originate from industry partners who propose problems and provide data sets.

The faculty adviser ensures the statistical integrity of the analysis, while the industry partner may provide domain context and feedback on the solution.

Publication expectation Students may work with proprietary data, but industry partners must agree that the thesis can be published and distributed.

Thesis timeline and commencement

Students begin planning thesis work before the second year and complete program milestones with faculty and program guidance.

1

Select a faculty adviser

Students should begin thinking about a faculty adviser and committee chair before the Fall quarter of the second year.

2

Nominate the master's committee

Students complete the Nomination of Master's Committee form at least the quarter before advancing to candidacy.

3

Advance to candidacy

The Advancement to Candidacy petition and unofficial transcripts are submitted within the first two weeks of the quarter the student plans to graduate.

4

File the thesis

Students send a complete draft to all committee members at least two months before the thesis filing deadline, then upload the final thesis for committee approval.

Departmental policy for June Commencement eligibility is to complete MASDS coursework and file the thesis by the end of the Summer immediately after Commencement.
