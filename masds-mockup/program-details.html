Program details

Graduate training in statistics, data science, and applied problem solving.

MASDS students complete at least 44 course units—seven required core courses and at least four electives—plus a written thesis based on original analysis.

44 unitsMinimum course credit required for the MASDS degree.
7 core coursesRequired graduate courses in statistical theory, methods, computing, data management, and communication.
4 electivesAt least four 4-unit electives chosen to support technical interests and professional goals.

Flexible pacing for working professionals

MASDS is designed for students who are balancing graduate study with professional responsibilities.

Complete in two yearsStudents who take two 4-unit courses per quarter will typically finish the program in spring of their second year. Students may take up to three courses in a single quarter to finish faster, but this heavy course load is not recommended for students who work full time.
Adjust when work gets busyStudents can take one course in heavier professional periods and continue moving through the program at a manageable pace.
Finish within program limitsStudents may take up to 10 regular quarters to complete the program without additional approval. Summer quarters do not count toward the 10-quarter limit, so this is about three years and one quarter. Students who need more time may be approved on a case-by-case basis.

Course waivers

Students may request waivers when they have already completed equivalent graduate-level coursework.

Up to three core coursesA maximum of three core courses may be waived by request.
Reviewed by the programThe Director, with consultation from the MASDS governance committee, reviews transcripts, syllabi, and other relevant materials.
44 units still requiredFor every core course waived, the student must complete additional elective coursework to satisfy the 44-unit degree requirement.

Thesis requirement

Every student completes original analysis on a real-world problem.

The MASDS thesis reports on research and analysis completed under the supervision of a UCLA faculty member and an industry partner when available. Thesis topics often originate from industry partners who propose problems and provide data sets.

The faculty advisor ensures the statistical integrity of the analysis, while the industry partner may provide domain context and feedback on the solution.

Students may work with proprietary data, but industry partners must agree that the thesis can be published and distributed.

Thesis timeline and commencement

Students begin planning thesis work before the second year and complete program milestones with faculty and program guidance.

1

Select a faculty advisor

Students should begin thinking about a faculty advisor and committee chair before they begin thesis work.

2

Nominate the master's committee

Students complete the Nomination of Master's Committee form at least the quarter before advancing to candidacy.

3

Advance to candidacy

The Advancement to Candidacy petition and unofficial transcripts are submitted within the first two weeks of the quarter the student plans to graduate.

4

File the thesis

Students send a complete draft to all committee members at least two months before the thesis filing deadline, then upload the final thesis for committee approval.

Departmental policy for June Commencement eligibility is to complete MASDS coursework and file the thesis by the end of the Summer immediately after Commencement.
